# Coin, Stashes & Supply
### Coin 
Coin is an abstract measure of cash and liquid assets such a jewelery and fine clothing. This is a rough value system, there are benefits to being high status, but it also makes you a bigger target as you can be captured and ransomed, or looted and killed.

Most daily transactions uses small silver balls for currency, commonly called ***shot*** or ***shrapnel***. This comes from soldiers loading them into their rifles when fighting undead.
They are also pretty easy for a forger to fake convinsingly. The silver pieces used in characters daily lives shouldn't be tracked. If a players wants to bribe a NPC, make an opposed ***status*** check against the NPC your trying to bribe to see if you have enough money on you to convince them.

##### Carrying Coin
Carrying more than 4 coin in ***shrapnel*** is impractical. You must spend the excess or put it in your stash (see below). A crew can also safely store up to 8 coin in their lair, no upgrades neccessary. If they upgrade to a vault, they can expand their stores to 16 and then 24 coin. Any coin beyond their stash limit must be spent before the next quest.

One unit of coin in silver pieces or other bulk currency takes up one item slot for your load when carried, or if carried as a ***crown*** then it doesn't take up space, but should still be noted for bribes.

##### Using Coins
- Spend ©1 to get an additional activity during downtime.
- Spend ©1 to increase the result level of a downtime activity roll.
- Spend ©1-3 to avoid certain crew entanglements.
- Put coin in your character’s stash to improve their lifestyle, and buy the class trappings required to improve.


### Stashes
##### Accessing your stash
If you want to remove coin out of your stash, you may do so, at a cost. When you sell your assets and investments quickly, you get 1 coin back in cash every for every 2 stash removed.

##### Status
The amount of coin your characters have stashed away determines their status. Track your stash on your crew sheet. 

- ©0-4 - Undesirable. You live in the streets, awash in vice and misery. 
- ©5-10 - Worker. You have a room you can keep things, although it isn't particularly secure.
- ©11-30 - Modest. A simple home or apartment, with some small comforts. You might operate a tavern or small business.
- ©30+ - Luxury. A well-appointed home or apartment, a few luxury items. You might own a medium business.
- Nobility. Not determined by wealth, but blood. Although, if you don't have a luxury lifestyle and evidence or reputation with the faction, you won't be believed. Unlocks a lot of doors.
