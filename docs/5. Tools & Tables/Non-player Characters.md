###### When creating NPC's try to give them all three of the following:
1. Drive: They should be motivated to do something
2. Impact: They should have the potential to permanently change the characters.
3. Flavour: They should be memorable. and speak to the flavour of the setting

This is about creating gameable NPC's in virtually no time that are immediately useful and enhance the quality of play. Avoid creating fleshed out backgrounds for NPC's and instead focus on how they are useful to your game. Instead, allow their depth and motivations to develop organically from play. This will create characters that are engaging to the players, as they will have naturally fleshed out the characters they like most, and breeze past the rest. 

##### What is the purpose of the non-player character?
An NPC can be used for any or all of the following: 
- distract the players
- provide a reward
- trigger an event
- trick or challenge the players
- cause penalties (i.e. empty, treasure, special, trick, or trap).

##### Reactions
The reaction roll is made on two six-sided dice. The results are usually that low (3-5) is hostile, average (6-8) are neutral and high (9-11) is friendly. There are usually special results on 2 and 12 (immediately attack and helpful). This roll is made when monsters are encountered, after the players decide whether or not to talk to the creature. Every NPC has a Reaction Roll Chart on the NPC side of their character card. 

##### How can you interact with them?
###### Attack
Starts an encounter
###### Avoid
Roll a dice for distance, near red, mid yellow, far green.
###### Bribe (Persuade)
If accepted, improves future rolls in the encounter. If Failed then increases in hostility
###### Command (Command)
Make an aggressive demands offering nothing in return
###### Grovel (Perform)
Check reaction and modify reaction  
###### Insult (Perform)
Lowers reaction of target, raises reaction of other groups, alternately lowers morale 
###### Question (Command)
Asks questions which are answered. Reaction determines helpfulness of answer 
###### Coerce (Force)
Reduces morale and improves compliance  
###### Sneak Attack (Sneak)
Starts surprise round, if there are survivors that recognise you roll a green die to gain [[dishonourable]] trait.
###### Hire
Check reaction to hire



### Additional Ideas
Having a few Non-Player Characters rolled up ahead of time popular professions for every faction can save a lot of time figuring things out at the table if their are players in your group that are easily distracted. We recommend asking about this in a Session 0.
