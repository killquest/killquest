Combat is deadly and incredibly dangerous and is usually best avoided, especially if you don't have an advantage or ***surprise***. It's also one of the most structured elements of play, this is how to resolve violent encounters:

Combat is deadly for the unprepared, it can come to a swift and violent end — without having to spend hours rolling dice to resolve a minor encounter! Violence will often lead to grievous injuries or death, so characters must be prepared and think carefully before drawing their weapons. 

##### How is it structured?
We split time into roughly 10 second intervals called rounds. This period of time is abstract and isn't really important. Everything within a round happens at basically the same time, and each character gets a turn within the round. When a character takes a turn, the player narrates what they are trying to do and the group decides what game mechanics can be used to best represent that action.

Go around the table and each describe how you react. Feel free to ask questions, and the group will decided on the consequences for any proposed actions. Once you understand the stakes and what you want to do, you commit to a course of action. Once everyone has done this, actions and ability saves are rolled, if anyone is taking Damage or a Wound, those are rolled. This repeats until the encounter is resolved.

###### Designers Note
We use alternate activations between the factions, so each 'enemy' would take a go, before the next faction would take their turn for one of their characters until each character has acted. A round is the time it takes for everyone to take a turn and the round ends. If one side has more combatants than the other, they continue taking turns until everyone has acted. 

##### Surprise!
Often encounters will start by one faction ambushing another. In this case, the surprised units must roll command checks. Those who succeed are able to react, those who fail to respond this round, and those who roll criticals get to go first.

##### How is it structured?
Combat takes place in both rounds and turns. A turn is focused on one character or unit. 

Each round is equal to roughly ten seconds of real time, with every unit’s turn happening roughly at the same time, although in turn order. Therefore, someone who is incapacitated before their turn does not get to act on their turn.
