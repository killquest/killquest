A social contract in the context of a role-playing game (RPG) is an agreement or set of guidelines established by the players where they willingly surrender some of their freedoms in order to ensure that the game remains enjoyable, fair, and respectful for everyone involved. It serves as a foundation for the group's collective experience, helping to create a positive and inclusive gaming environment.

Feel free to adapt the social contract to your specific group's needs and preferences. It should serve as a foundation for open communication and a collaborative spirit to ensure everyone has a great time playing the game.

##### The Contract
In our role-playing game, we recognize the importance of mutual respect, cooperation, and fairness to ensure that everyone has an enjoyable and rewarding experience. This social contract outlines the principles and expectations that will guide our interactions and gameplay. By agreeing to these terms, we aim to create a fun, inclusive, and immersive RPG experience for all participants.
###### Respect Others
We commit to treating each other with respect, out of character. This means avoiding derogatory language and actions that could make others uncomfortable. Disagreements should be resolved respectfully and constructively.
###### Establish Boundaries
We agree to establish boundaries and ensure a shared understanding for themes such as violence, romance, or other potentially uncomfortable themes and respect each player's comfort level. 
We have a theme checklist so you can find out what themes are in your game, or create your own checklist in a session zero for homebrew games.
###### Agree on Authority
The group has the responsibility of creating and running the game world. We agree to respect the rulings of the group, even if they differ from established rules, and address philosophical disputes after the session to avoid disrupting gameplay. We acknowledge that the game may evolve, and rules or expectations may need adjusting as we go. We are open to change if it enhances our collective enjoyment.
###### Choices Matter
Every player should have the opportunity to contribute to the story and make meaningful choices for their characters. We will strive to share the spotlight and ensure that no player is consistently left out or overshadowed.
###### Pacing and Engagement
We will be attentive to pacing, striving to maintain a balance between storytelling, role-playing, combat, and exploration, according to the group's preferences. Open and honest communication is essential. If any player has concerns or suggestions, they are encouraged to voice them constructively. The group should actively listen and work as a team to find solutions.
###### Inclusivity
All players are encouraged to contribute to the story, create opportunities for each other, and engage with the world creatively. When being creative we will be mindful of different perspectives and experiences, and strive to create a welcoming space for all players. 
###### Conclusion
By committing to this social contract, we agree to creating an enjoyable experience for everyone. We understand that each of us plays a vital role in fostering a fun, fair, and inclusive gaming environment. Let's embark on our adventures with respect, creativity, and a shared commitment to making this RPG memorable for all.

##### When to Roll
When improvising you will likely reach a point in the story where the outcome is uncertain. We have designed our core gameplay loop around solving almost action with a simple idea, a chart and some dice. 

- Don't roll if the characters are doing something that you’d expect them to easily accomplish. 
You also automatically succeed on a roll that is two ranks below you.

This is encouraged to speed up games.

Each group will have their own ideas about what "uncertain" means. This is a useful way to tweak the difficulty, and establish the tone and style of your games. This should be established in a session zero.
