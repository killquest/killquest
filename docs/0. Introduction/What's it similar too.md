### What is it similar too?
Here's a list of games you should definitely try that I owe a huge debt to for coming up with some fantastic rules that I have adapted to create KillQuest:
- **Blades in the Dark** for factions & actions
- **Dead of Winter** for Dice & Objectives
- **Knave** for quick and flexible characters
- **Mausritter** for inventive inventories
- **Maze Rats** for endlessly useful tables
- **Mordheim** for terrain and immersion
- **Mothership** for layout and 
- **Oath** for legacy and situation mechanics
- **RootRPG** for characters & playbooks
- **Star Wars Legion** for punchy combat

If you need inspiration to create content for RPG's then these are some of my most inspiring muses.
- **Slowquest** for maps, props & questhooks
- **Ben Milton** for cataloguing the OSR
- **The Old World** for being charmingly dark
- **A Song of Ice & Fire** for it’s rich world
- **Lord of the Rings** for creating fantasy
- **MCDM** for teaching how to run RPG’s
- **RP Archive** for magnetic modular terrain
- **Map Crow** for mapping & world building

This is by no means an extensive list, there are so many fantastic creators out there producing exceptional and inspiring content. I am trying to pull from my love of all games, whether board games, war games and more. I have a list of over 30 games I studied to find mechanics, and read, watched and played even more trying to figure out what I want.

Two of my biggest inspiration is Bodieh from Slowquest and Ben from Questing Beast without whom KillQuest wouldn’t exist as they both reignited my interest in running D&D and taught me how to do it without burning out on being a DM.
