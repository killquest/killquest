Playing KillQuest is just like having a conversation the world, the characters and the things that might happen. There are no rules to say whose turn it is to talk, instead players follow the natural flow of the conversation, which has some back-and-forth. Players ask questions or make statements and then the group works out what might happens next, together. It is never a monologue lead by a single person; it’s always a conversation within a group.

The rules also help shape the conversation of play. While the players are talking, the rules and the fiction are talking, too. Every rule has an explicit fictional trigger that tells you when it is meant to come into the conversation. Like any conversation, the time you spend listening is just as important as the time you spend talking. The details established by the other people at the table are important to you: they might change what moves you can make, set up an opportunity for you, or create a challenge you have to face. The conversation works best when we all listen, ask questions, and build on each other’s contributions.

When improvising you will likely reach a point in the story where the outcome is uncertain. We have designed our core gameplay loop around solving almost action with a simple idea, a chart and the resolution dice. Each group will have their own ideas about what "uncertain" means. This is a useful way to tweak the difficulty, and establish the tone and style of your games. This should be established in a session zero. 

##### Structured
Sometimes you need the game to be a bit more structured, and have the world be a little more consistent so the players have a good idea of their consequences. You might be in a fight and need to work out how to use the environment to your advantage.
