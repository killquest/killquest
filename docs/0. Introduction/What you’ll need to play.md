- Four six-sided dice per player, two each of black and white. You really just need two different colours you can use to denote success and failure − I’ll use black and white for clarity.
* Several dozen index cards or sticky notes and some pencils.
* About two and a half hours, varying with experience,



There are a few things you will need before you can play. The first is a few friends, typically between three and five of you is ideal, but we've play with as few as two and as many as six. You will also need something to write notes, character cards and some dice, more on them later. 

Warlock is a role-playing game with a narrative emphasis, so you do not strictly need miniatures, maps or terrain. Saying that, we highly recommend you play this way, just don't let what you see limit your the final thing you have to bring, your imagination.


###### Playing Surface
Firstly, you will need a flat area approximately 1m by 1m to play your battles on. Any firm, level surface is fine, kitchen tables will work well enough. A simple solution is to use a themed neoprene mat to provide some decoration and protect the table from scratches. Our preferred method is a custom built gaming board. It is unbeatable for immersion, and feels epic but is not necessary for play.
###### Dice
KillQuest uses three types of dice; a twelve sided dice (d12), a twenty sided dice (d20) and a six sided dice (d6). You may be asked to roll a number of dice in one go. For example, 2d6 means roll two six sided dice and add their scores together (i.e. 3+4+7). 

###### Stat Cards
Print out some stat cards on 6x4 inch index cards. We print out a lot of information and statistic cards on this size as they are easy to store in binders. All you need is polypockets for standard photos (6x4 inch).
###### Models
Players, generally, find a table filled with things they can touch and move around to be much more interactive and engaging. I find that being able to see what you are doing and keep track of who is where is makes games significantly easier to follow for those who are new to tabletop gaming. 

If you've only got a few hours between sessions then I'd go for printing papercraft card designs. If you've got more of a budget and plenty of time then I'd recommend painting miniatures, as it can't be beaten for impact. Each player should have enough miniatures to represent every character in their cult. Ideally you would have a model for ever NPC's you expect to encounter too. 

###### Terrain
The adventures of KillQuest take place in taverns, city streets, sewers, forests and more. In a pinch, a stack of books or a few upside-down cups can serve as terrain, provided that each player uses a bit of imagination. Although pre-cut MDF and plastic scenery are available from a variety of companies for easy and reasonably affordable solution.

However, many hobbyists enjoy making their own. We have our own system of modular terrain pieces that allows for easy storage, destructible terrain and easy reusability. We think this is a really neat solution. Check out more of our crafting philosophy on our blog.

###### Measuring Devices
We have four templates used to determine movement, range, and damage zones. If that's not available to you, you can also use a tape measure or ruler.
