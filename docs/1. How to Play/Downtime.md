Downtime fulfils two purposes in the game:
1. First, it’s a break for the players. During the action of a Quest, the characters are charging from obstacle to obstacle in a high-energy sequence. Downtime gives them a reprieve so they can catch their breath and relax a bit—focus on lower-energy, quieter elements of the game, as well as explore personal aspects of their characters.
2. Second, the shift into a new phase of the game signals a shift in which mechanics are needed. There are special rules that are only used during the downtime phase, so they’re kept “out of the way” during the other parts of play. When we shift into downtime, we take out a different toolbox and resolve downtime on its own terms, then shift back into the more action-focused phases of the game afterwards.

Downtime, basically is a more open world setting where you are not in an encounter.

Downtime is divided into four parts, which are resolved in order:

1. Payoff. The crew share their treasure and loot and store it in their safe, with the crew putting their shares in the safehouse
2. Heat. The crew updates notoriety and the bounty from the law as a result of their last job. 
3. Entanglements. The crew faces trouble from the rival factions, the law, and the haunted city itself.
4. Downtime Activities. The PCs indulge their vices to remove stress, work on long-term projects, recover from injuries, etc.

After the downtime activities are resolved, the game returns to free play, and the group can move toward their next score. 

###### Vices
Engaging in vices allows you to deal with ***Stress*** without removing your character from play. Although it does remove stress, it is not a fix all solution.
