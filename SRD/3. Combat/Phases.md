##### Gambit Phase
1. ***Choose a Gambit***: Players select a gambit card from their hand and place it face down on the table. Then, all players simultaneously reveal their cards. 
*Non-Player Factions have a card drawn at random from their pile instead.*
2. ***Determine Activation Order***: The player who spent the most Influence goes first, then go in decreasing influence, until the Activation order is settled.
3. ***Issue Initiative***: In order, each player gives initiative tokens. The number of tokens to be issued is indicated on the gambit card.
4. ***Create Unit Deck***: Each player puts all of the followers they did not give orders too into a Unit Deck, and then shuffle them.

##### Activation Phase
In initiative order players take turns activating their units in the following sequence:
1. Activate the Unit: Either choose a unit with orders or draw from your unit deck. Once activated roll up their action dice. 
2. Perform Actions: use action or influence dice, and any free actions you may have.
3. Resolve Unit: Once the phase is completed, places the unit’s order card in the exhausted pile and resolve any tokens and effects. This included any effects that say “at the end of this characters activation”

##### Resolution Phase
1. Each player discards their revealed command card, it cannot be used again this encounter.
2. Resolve all tokens and effects. This included any effects that say “at the end of the turn”
3. Resolve Events. Roll 3 red dice, and draw how many pips are showing. Also draw any pre-planned time based event cards. Do what is written on the card and then discard.
4. Clear Exhausted Deck. Re-add exhausted cards back into their decks.
 
During the resolution phase you pull out an event card. This decides how the round will end. This is like a chance card in Monopoly. You may gain resupplies, a free attack etc, the weather takes a turn for the worse.

During the resolution phase you reset all tokens, resolve all unresolved actions such as working out critical wounds. Once that is done, pull out an event card. This decides how the round will end. This is like a chance card in Monopoly. You may gain resupplies, a free attack etc, the weather takes a turn for the worse, an enemy shows up, and challenges you, etc. Then at the end of the phase you roll your Action Dice for the next turn.
