I see the traditional role of a Game Master as closer to a Director, and I believe the role of what a Game Master is varies from table to table. So I've broken them down into types of what I will refer to as Hobby Heroes. Personally I believe in sharing the responsibility and giving players more agency. 

You might be the only person in the game group that’s read the book — that’s fine. What the other players will need to do, though, is buy into the idea of the game. Everyone should have at least read the Players Toolkit, as well as having one on the table for quick reference. 

If their not excited, then this game probably isn’t for them. That’s fine. If they are then we’d recommend a quick read through our guide to the world to get a good handle on the important places and factions involved, what they want, who runs them. 

A brief understanding of these can make for a much more interesting campaign. You don’t want everyone sitting there, excited to play, and then say, “So... what do you want to do first?” The starting situation keeps the momentum high at the beginning.

### Director
As a facilitator, you’re job is to introduce the concepts of the world and the game, explain examples if needed, and give some light feedback to keep everyone focused. If someone has gotten off track or misunderstood a point, it should be easy to course-correct. As long as you’re kind, clear, and quick with the correction, your fellow players will appreciate your guidance! We're trying to encourage excitement and creativity while maintaining respect and trust, we want to create a safe and fun place where people can offer a lot of ideas and then work together to make our ideas even better.

Some directors, like Tarantino or Jon Favreau, are directors and actors in their movies. I believe the roll of DM can be shared amongst all players, whilst one takes the lead as director but they can also influence the tone and genre more as a player. This is to be avoided if you have an ego or get jealous, as it's all too easy to become the centre of your own story and force your players to listen to a monologue of all your ideas.
