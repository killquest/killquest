The first step of creating a story is for everyone playing the game to collaboratively decide on a Faction. Your choice of Faction will strongly influence your experience, and every player should be involved. We'd recommend all players working for the same faction for your first few games as it streamlines play having all players, at least roughly, working together. 

The element that binds player characters together is the shared loyalty to a faction. This common purpose is what unites the players and gives a strong foundation from which they can explore the world and play the faction game.

Ultimately, the factions are, in many ways, another character, but it is one controlled by all the players and their actions. It has a history, a place, and a function. It has quantifiable attributes that reflect its strengths and weakness, and it can grow and thrive or wither and die. But for as much as a faction is integral to the players, it also stands apart, functioning in the background as the player characters carve out their places in history. The house has lived long before the players, and unless disaster strikes, it will live long after they are all dust.

Having players work for different factions is a lot of fun and a good way to add challenge to the game once all players are experienced in the mechanics. The potential for backstabbing and more in depth politics does make learning the game a lot harder.

##### How Important is a Faction?
Some games may focus almost entirely on the faction, and the characters are unimportant in comparison. In such games, each player might control their own house and have a roster of characters they might use to facilitate the interests of their house and its survival, and when game play focuses on characters, it does so using only those characters pertinent to the greater story of the campaign.

Some games, especially when playing for the first time, won't use the faction system, instead focusing on a more traditional RPG style of play and follow a group of freelancing adventurers with no ties to the world.

Most games, though, take a middle-ground approach, where the players interact with the setting with a handful of characters, with all players united under a house. While the fate of the house is important, is not as vital as the development of the individual characters and the unfolding of their stories.

##### Choosing a Faction
What factions you work for helps to shape the missions and plots your party will undertake. We have number of pre-made factions so you can get straight into game, however a lot of fun can be had making your own, we go into this in our Tools & Tables section (P.XX).

Choosing a Faction is a collaborative process between the players. Your patron will have very different goals, depending on what part of the Imperium they represent, and this will influence the missions you undertake, the enemies you face and even the resources at your disposal. 

### Patrons
You are a Patron, an unseen force guiding your party. You are in the background of every scene influencing events in their favour using your magick and knowledge of the world to ensure their success in completing your plots. Patron's advance their Faction’s goals, but also serve their own agendas. A patrons precise goals and methods will vary wildly, and may occasionally even be at odds with others in your faction.  

##### Creating a Patron
You’ll need to decide what motivates you, what are your advantages and liabilities, and how much influence you really have. 

##### Reputation
Reputation is a currency, and while characters may accrue some connections and influence of their own, their Faction operates at a far higher level and will influence how characters are seen.

You will have relationships with other factions and that ebb and flow during the course of the game — often because of character actions during missions. Friendly factions might provide assistance to your characters, while those that hold a grudge against the Faction are likely to be suspicious of anyone acting in their name.

##### Boons and Liabilities
One of the most important steps of Patron creation is choosing their **Boons** and **Liabilities**. 

Patron Boons are powerful abilities that can be drawn upon between or during missions. Well-armed patrons may provide their servants with the use of rare and precious weapons, while more subtle individuals may provide false identities to allow the party to operate undercover. 

However, for every Boon a Patron enjoys, the GM is allowed to secretly choose a Liability. This might be an old enemy, a rival faction, or a dedication to the Emperor that borders on the obsessive. Whatever the case might be, these liabilities are sure to come back to haunt the players.
