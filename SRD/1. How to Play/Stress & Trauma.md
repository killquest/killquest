##### Stress
Stress represents the accumulation of completing strenuous activities or pushing yourself beyond what you would normally be capable of. The more stress you accrue, the higher the chance you have of it ticking over into something serious when you ***Panic***. Whilst stress doesn't do anything itself, the higher your stress, the more likely you are to ***Panic***. 

##### Pushing Yourself
When characters suffer a consequence that they don’t want to accept, they can take stress instead. How much stress they take is equal to the difficulty of the task they are trying to re-roll.




##### What is a Panic Check?
It's a test to see if your characters can keep their cool under pressure. 

You must roll 2d6 on a Panic table.

##### When should I roll for Panic?
Roll for panic when a character:
- Loses more than half their max health in one hit
- Sees a crew-mate die
- See multiple crew-mates panic at the same time
- Enters combat with a ***Un-natural*** creature

##### Trauma
Eventually the life of an Adventurer will take it's toll, and bring your characters to their breaking point. When that happens there's a chance you will lose control and Panic.
